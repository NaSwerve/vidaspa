Fibroblast

SKIN TIGHTENING • LONG-LASTING

1hr 30mins

Fibroblast treatment, is a non-surgical cosmetic procedure that uses a pen-like device to deliver targeted electrical currents to the skin. This process creates tiny, controlled micro-injuries, stimulating the skin's natural healing response and promoting the production of collagen and elastin.

The treatment experience

Cleansing the skin and applying a topical anesthetic (numbing) cream. Then we wait about 30 minutes for the numbing cream to take effect.


Treating the designated skin area with the plasma pen. The pen will create small arcs of microcurrents that make small scab-like dots on the skin.


Our professional nurse will remove the numbing cream and apply a cooling gel to minimize the tingling and burning sensation whenever possible.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
After fibroblast treatment, you can expect some redness, swelling, and tiny crusts or scabs on the treated area. These are normal side effects that usually resolve within a few days to a couple of weeks. The treated skin may also feel like a mild sunburn and appear dry or flaky as it heals. You'll also likely see immediate skin tightening and lifting, with continued improvements over the following weeks and months.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Post treatment Do not pick scabs! Removing the scabs prematurely may result in scarring and discoloration. Allow scabs to come off on their own. This will ensure the best possible result and avoid scarring, hyperpigmentation and infection. If you feel the scabs are accidentally being rubbed off prematurely, then protect them with by covering them with Aquaphor to prevent falling off too early. The longer the scabs stay put the longer the skin has a chance to heal itself with the protection of the scab.


Ice the treated area using a clean towel to cover the ice pack each use. Alternate 10 minutes on and off for up to 1 hour for the first 2 days.


Do not apply makeup (even mineral makeup) until the scabbing has fallen off completely. This is to avoid infection or disrupting the scabs prematurely.


Common questions

What does the fibroblast treatment involve?

+

The Plasma Fibroblast Skin Tightening treatment involves using a hand-held, electronic medical device that generates a plasma charge, or arc, from the ions in the air between the tip of the device and the surface of the skin. The energy is then transferred into the skin and activates the fibroblasts in the dermis to repair. At the point where the plasma arc touches the skin, a tiny carbon dot is produced due to the thermal reaction of the skin, and the skin around this dot contracts. When many small dots are created in an optimal grid-like pattern in a targeted area, the whole area contracts resulting in a visible tightening of the skin.

What are Fibroblasts?

+

Fibroblasts are special cells in the dermis layer of the skin whose main function is to produce tropocollagen that fills the spaces between cells and fibres in connective tissue. They appear to play an important part in wound healing, as following tissue injury, they migrate to the site of damage where they deposit new collagen and facilitate the healing process.

How many treatments will I need?

+

In many cases only 1 treatment is needed, however for this depends on skin type and your desired level of correction. For deeper lines or looser skin, 2 or 3 treatments may be required.

What do I need to do prior to the procedure?

+

You may like to plan events around your procedure accordingly, as the treated areas of skin will have a pattern of small carbon dots which will remain there for 7-10 days. Makeup cannot be applied for the first 5 days. We suggest arriving to your appointment with clean, makeup-free skin.

Is it painful?

+

As the procedure is non-invasive, the level of pain is minimal but you will experience a mild discomfort. We also use a topical anaesthetic to make the procedure as comfortable as possible. It's important to recognise that everyone has a different level of tolerance to pain, which can also be affected by personal circumstances including whether you are a smoker, are taking certain medications or even where you are in your menstrual cycle.
