The treatment experience

Laser Genesis Procedure: The skin is cleansed, and protective eyewear is provided for the patient. The Laser Genesis handpiece is gently moved across the treatment area, delivering laser energy to the skin. This process is typically described as comfortable, with patients often feeling a warming sensation.

Carboxytherapy Procedure: The treatment area is cleansed, and the patient may be offered a topical anesthetic to minimize discomfort. A fine needle is used to inject medical-grade CO2 gas into the subcutaneous or intradermal layers of the skin.

Benefits:

  • Enhanced Collagen Production - Both treatments stimulate collagen production, leading to firmer, more youthful-looking skin.
  • Improved Skin Tone and Texture - Carboxytherapy can improve blood circulation and oxygenation, while Laser Genesis can address skin imperfections, leading to a more even skin tone and smoother texture.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
After your treatment, expect some redness and swelling, which are normal and should subside within a few hours to a few days. There is minimal downtime, allowing you to return to most daily activities immediately. You may also experience some dryness, which can be managed with moisturizers.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before undergoing Laser Genesis and Co2 Carboxytherapy avoiding sun exposure and tanning beds for at least two weeks, discontinuing the use of certain skincare products like retinoids and exfoliants, and arriving with clean, makeup-free skin.


After Laser Genesis and Co2 CarboxyTherapy, protect your skin from the sun, avoid harsh skincare products, and refrain from strenuous activities or excessive heat for a few days. Specifically, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher, avoid retinoids, exfoliants, and alcohol-based toners, and skip saunas, hot tubs, and intense workouts.

Common questions

How does the Laser Genesis +Co2 CarboxyTherapy work?

+

Laser Genesis and Carboxytherapy are both cosmetic procedures that can be used to improve skin appearance, and they can be combined for enhanced results. Laser Genesis stimulates collagen production to reduce wrinkles and improve skin texture. Carboxytherapy involves injecting carbon dioxide to boost circulation and collagen production, which can help with scars, stretch marks, and skin tightening.

What are the risks?

+

Some side effects of Laser Genesis include swelling and redness, but these symptoms usually disappear within 30 to 60 minutes of treatment. As a low-risk procedure, it can produce skin results within three to four weeks, and the best results occur after three to six treatments.

How many treatments will I need?

+

The number of Laser Genesis and CO2 CarboxyTherapy treatments needed varies, but typically 3-6 sessions are recommended for Laser Genesis, and 1-3 for CO2 laser treatments, depending on the individual's skin condition and desired results. Consultations with a dermatologist or aesthetician are crucial to determine the ideal treatment plan for your specific needs.

Is it painful?

+

Laser Genesis is generally considered a painless procedure, while Carboxytherapy can cause some mild discomfort or a warming sensation, but is generally well-tolerated. Topical numbing cream is often used to minimize any potential discomfort during Carboxytherapy, especially when treating sensitive areas.
