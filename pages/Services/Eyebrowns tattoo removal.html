The treatment experience

Numbing creams are often used to minimize discomfort during the procedure, and common over-the-counter pain relievers can help manage any post-treatment soreness. The process involves breaking down the tattoo ink with a laser, which can cause some redness, swelling, and scabbing, but these side effects are typically temporary.

Benefits:

  • Correcting Mistakes: Eyebrow tattoos can be the result of poor technique, outdated methods, or simply a change in personal preference. Removal allows for correcting these issues and achieving a more desirable look.
  • Restoring Natural Appearance: Eyebrow tattoos can fade, blur, or change color over time, leading to an unnatural look. Removal can help restore the natural shape and color of the brows.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
You can expect some redness, swelling, and tenderness in the treated area, similar to a sunburn. Some individuals might also experience pinpoint bleeding or blistering. These immediate effects usually subside within a few hours to a few days. Over the next few weeks, the treated area may develop scabs as part of the healing process, and the tattoo pigment will gradually fade as your body absorbs it. The pigment may change color during the process. And some hair may be descolored as well, but it will return to normal color as it regrows.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Pre-tattoo removal, avoid sun exposure and self-tanning products, retinol or active exfoliants, and blood-thinning medications or alcohol.


Post-tattoo removal, keep the area clean and dry, avoid sun exposure, and don't pick at scabs or blisters.

Common questions

What methods are used for eyebrow tattoo removal?

+

Laser removal: The most common method, using laser technology to break down the tattoo pigment.


Saline removal: An alternative method that uses a saline solution to lift the pigment.


Natural methods (lemon juice, creams, etc.): Generally not effective for permanent tattoos.

Will there be scarring?

+

Laser removal, when performed correctly by qualified professionals, carries a low risk of scarring.


Following aftercare instructions is crucial to minimize the risk of scarring.


How many treatments will I need?

+

The number of sessions varies based on factors such as tattoo depth, ink color, and individual skin response, but typically ranges from 2 to 10 sessions for laser removal. Some tattoos may fade more quickly than others. The number of sessions will depend on the technique used to apply the ink, as well as the quality of the ink and the age of your tattoo.


Is it painful?

+

Some discomfort is expected, but numbing cream can be applied to minimize pain.


Side effects like redness, swelling, and scabbing are common but typically subside within a few days.


Hair discoloration in some cases.
