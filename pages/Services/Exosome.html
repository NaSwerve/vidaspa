The treatment experience

Exosomes can be applied to the skin's surface in the form of creams, serums, or masks. The exosomes are gently massaged into the skin, allowing them to penetrate and communicate with the cells in the epidermis and dermis.

Microneedling create microchannels on the skin's surface, and the exosomes can be applied topically and then infused into the skin through these microchannels, enhancing their absorption and effectiveness.

For more targeted and intensive rejuvenation, exosomes can be injected directly into specific areas of the face. This technique allows for the precise delivery of exosomes to deeper layers of the skin, stimulating collagen production and promoting overall skin rejuvenation.

Benefits:

  • Hair Growth - For individuals experiencing hair loss or thinning, exosome therapy can stimulate hair follicles, promoting hair growth and improving hair density.
  • Tissue Repair and Regeneration - Exosomes can promote the repair of damaged tissues, including those affected by injuries or chronic conditions. They can stimulate cell growth and regeneration, aiding in the healing of wounds and improving overall tissue health.
  • Immune Modulation - Exosomes play a role in immune regulation, potentially helping to reduce inflammation and promote a healthy immune response.
  • Wound Healing - Exosomes can accelerate the healing of wounds, including chronic wounds, by promoting cell proliferation and migration.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
After exosome therapy, you can generally expect minimal downtime and a gradual improvement in the treated area. Mild side effects like redness, swelling, or bruising at the injection site are possible but usually subside quickly. The full benefits of the therapy, such as skin rejuvenation or hair regrowth, may take several weeks or months to become noticeable as the exosomes work to stimulate cellular repair and regeneration.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before undergoing exosome therapy avoid certain medications and supplements like blood thinners, stay hydrated, and preparing your skin by cleansing it thoroughly before the procedure.


After exosome therapy, avoid strenuous activities, refrain from anti-inflammatory medications, and protect the treated area from heat and sunlight. Additionally, maintaining proper hygiene, hydration, and a healthy diet is important for optimal recovery.

Common questions

How does the Ultraformer work?

+

Exosome therapy utilizes exosomes, which are tiny vesicles released by cells, to facilitate cell-to-cell communication and promote tissue regeneration. These exosomes act as messengers, carrying proteins and genetic material to target cells, stimulating healing and rejuvenation processes within the body. In essence, exosome therapy harnesses the body's natural healing mechanisms to address various conditions, including skin rejuvenation, joint issues, and even potential benefits in neurodegenerative diseases.

What are the risks?

+

Exosome therapy, while promising, does carry some potential risks. These include local reactions like redness, swelling, and mild inflammation at the injection site, as well as potential allergic reactions. In some cases, patients may experience temporary fatigue or a slight feeling of warmth.

How many treatments will I need?

+

2 to 4 sessions are recommended, spaced 2-4 weeks apart. Visible results can often be seen after the first or second session, with effects potentially lasting for several months.

Is it painful?

+

Exosome therapy is generally considered a minimally invasive and low-pain procedure. While some patients may experience mild discomfort or a slight stinging sensation during the treatment, especially during microneedling, it's usually minimized with the application of numbing cream. Any pain or discomfort after the procedure is typically minimal and short-lived, with some soreness or tenderness at the injection site being the most common experience.
