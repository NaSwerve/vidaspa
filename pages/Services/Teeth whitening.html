The treatment experience

Our professional nurse applies a whitening gel, often containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, to the teeth and then activates it with a light, such as a blue LED light. The light helps the peroxide penetrate the tooth enamel and break down stains, resulting in a brighter smile.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
After your treatment, you can expect some temporary sensitivity and potential gum irritation, which should subside within a few days.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before teeth whitening, avoid stain-causing foods and drinks like coffee, tea, red wine, and berries.


Refrain from eating for at least one hour; water is ok.


Post-whitening, continue avoiding any colorful foods for 24 hours, and also be cautious with acidic foods and smoking. Use desensitizing toothpaste and follow your expert's specific instructions.

Common questions

Is teeth whitening safe?

+

Yes, teeth whitening is generally safe.

Does it damage teeth?

+

Professional whitening does not damage teeth when done correctly.

What are the risks?

+

Teeth whitening procedures are generally safe. Common side effects include tooth sensitivity and gum irritation, while more serious issues like enamel erosion and damage to dental restorations can occur.

How many treatments will I need?

+

One to three sessions are required to have optimum results.

Will it work on all types of stains?

+

Whitening works best on extrinsic stains (caused by food, drinks, and smoking). Intrinsic stains (those caused by medication or dental trauma) may be more challenging to lighten with whitening alone.
