The treatment experience

Laser hair removal is a cosmetic procedure that uses concentrated light (lasers) to target and destroy hair follicles, reducing or eliminating hair growth. It's a popular method for long-term hair reduction.

What to Know About The Laser

Technology: Alma Soprano devices use Super Hair Removal (SHR) technology, which gradually heats the dermis to effectively damage hair follicles and prevent regrowth, while protecting surrounding tissue. The Soprano ICE Platinum and Soprano Titanium utilize a triple-cluster diode technology, combining three wavelengths (755nm Alexandrite, 810nm Diode, and 1064nm YAG) for a comprehensive approach targeting different tissue depths within the hair follicle, resulting in improved efficacy for all skin and hair types, including fine and light hairs.


Benefits:

  • Virtually painless: The devices feature integrated cooling systems like ICE™ technology that minimizes discomfort, making the procedure virtually painless.
  • Safe for all skin types: Alma Soprano is considered safe and effective for all skin types (Fitzpatrick Skin Types I-VI), including dark and tanned skin, unlike many traditional laser systems.
  • Effective for various hair types and colors: The combination of wavelengths targets different hair thicknesses and pigments, making it effective for a wider range of hair colors (excluding white hair due to lack of melanin).
  • Faster treatment times: The in-motion technique, covering a larger area with each pulse, results in quicker treatment sessions.
  • Long-lasting results: While not entirely permanent, Alma Soprano hair removal offers long-lasting hair reduction, with many patients achieving up to 90% hair removal after the recommended number of sessions.

What to Expect:

  • Sessions typically last 5-45 minutes, depending on the treatment area.
  • The treated area should be shaved and free of lotions or makeup.
  • You may feel a warming sensation, but discomfort is minimal due to the cooling technology.
  • Side effects are usually none to mild, including redness or sensitivity, which typically resolve quickly.
  • Multiple sessions (typically 4-12) are required, spaced 4-8 weeks apart, depending on the area treated.

Important Considerations:

  • Maintenance: While results are long-lasting, periodic maintenance sessions may be needed.

Benefits:

  • Permanent hair reduction: Laser hair removal can significantly reduce hair growth over time. While it's not always perfectly permanent, many people experience a long-lasting reduction in hair, with some even achieving permanent hair removal.
  • Reduced Ingrown Hairs: By damaging or destroying hair follicles, laser hair removal can prevent future ingrown hairs, a common problem associated with methods like shaving or waxing.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Long-term savings from not needing to purchase razors, shaving cream, or waxing appointments can make it a more cost-effective option in the long run.
  • Improved Skin Texture: Laser hair removal can lead to smoother, clearer skin in the treated areas as it eliminates ingrown hairs and reduces the need for frequent shaving or waxing.

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
You'll likely notice hair shedding within 1-3 weeks as targeted hairs exit the follicles.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before the treatment: Shave the area a day or two before the treatment to allow for some stubble but avoid long hairs.


Please stop taking any nails and hair growth supplements at least a month before for optimum results.


After the treatment: Avoid sun exposure. Keep the area hydrated. Avoid other hair removal methods.

Common questions

How does it work?

+

Laser hair removal uses concentrated light beams to target melanin (the pigment in hair).


The light energy is converted to heat, damaging the hair follicle and inhibiting future growth.


This process is most effective when hair is in its active growth phase (anagen), which is why multiple treatments are needed to target follicles at different growth stages.

Is it permanent?

+

Laser hair removal significantly reduces hair growth and can make hair finer and lighter, but it's not always a permanent solution. And some touch-ups may be required.


Hormonal fluctuations can affect hair regrowth, and some individuals may experience some hair regrowth over time or may require more sessions to achieve the desired results.


Results vary per person, and some may achieve 100% hair reduction while others may see some fine hairs grow back.

What are the risks?

+

Laser hair removal is generally considered safe when performed by a trained and qualified professional.


Certain medications, skin conditions, and medical history may make someone ineligible for treatment.


Let us know of any known allergies.

How many treatments will I need?

+

Most people need multiple laser hair removal sessions to achieve optimal results. Typically, 6-12 sessions spaced several weeks apart are recommended, depending on the area treated.
