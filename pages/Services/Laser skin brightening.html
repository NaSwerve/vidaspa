The treatment experience

The treatment area is cleansed, and topical anesthetic may be applied to minimize discomfort. The dermatologist uses a handheld Pico laser device to deliver ultra-short energy pulses to the targeted areas. The treated area is cleaned, and a soothing ointment is applied. Sun protection is crucial, and the VidaSpaNYC will provide aftercare instructions.

Benefits:

Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
After your treatment, you can expect some redness, swelling, and a sunburn-like sensation in the treated area, which are normal reactions as the skin heals. The skin may also feel warm or sensitive. These effects are usually temporary, with redness and swelling subsiding within a few days. As the skin heals, it may also peel or flake, and some darkening of pigmentation spots can occur initially before they fade.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before a laser skin brightening treatment avoid sun exposure, tanning beds, and self-tanners for several weeks beforehand. You should also stop using certain skincare products like retinol and hydroquinone a few days before the procedure. Additionally, it's important to discontinue the use of blood-thinning medications and supplements, and to avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.


After laser skin brightening treatment, it's crucial to protect your skin from sun exposure, avoid harsh skincare products, and refrain from activities that can irritate the treated area. Specifically, you should avoid direct sunlight for several weeks, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with high SPF, and skip products containing retinol, glycolic acid, or alcohol. Also, avoid touching or picking at the treated area and refrain from heavy workouts or hot environments that could cause sweating or irritation.

Common questions

How does the Ultraformer work?

+

Laser skin brightening treatment works by using focused light energy to target and break down excess melanin, the pigment responsible for dark spots and uneven skin tone. The laser selectively heats and fragments the melanin, allowing the body to naturally remove it, leading to a brighter and more even complexion. Additionally, the treatment can stimulate collagen production, which contributes to overall skin rejuvenation and a youthful appearance.

What are the benefits?

+

Laser skin brightening can improve skin tone, reduce hyperpigmentation (sunspots, melasma), and improve overall skin texture.

What are the risks?

+

Laser skin brightening is generally considered safe when performed by qualified professionals. However, like any cosmetic procedure, it has potential side effects.


Ultraformer is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to the ultrasound technology used.

How many treatments will I need?

+

The number of laser skin brightening treatments needed varies, but most people see noticeable improvements after 2-4 sessions spaced a few weeks to months apart. The exact number depends on individual skin type, the severity of the condition being treated, and desired results.

How long do results last?

+

Results can last for several months to years, depending on factors like sun exposure, skincare routine, and individual skin type.
