Pre and post care

What can I expect after my treatment?

+
Common side effects of intimate bleaching may include temporary redness, itching, or mild irritation. In rare cases, more serious complications such as skin infections, scarring, or allergic reactions may occur.

What precautions should I take pre and post?

+

Before Procedure

Consult a professional: Seek advice from a dermatologist or qualified aesthetician to determine if the treatment is suitable for your skin type and to understand the potential risks and benefits.


Avoid waxing or shaving: Refrain from waxing or shaving the area for a few days before the procedure to prevent irritation and potential complications.


Avoid chemical peels: Do not undergo chemical peels or other skin-lightening treatments in the same area before bleaching.


After Procedure

Avoid sexual intercourse: Abstain from sexual activity for at least a week to allow the skin to heal and minimize the risk of infection.


Avoid hot baths and swimming: Steer clear of hot baths, hot tubs, and swimming pools for a week to prevent irritation and potential infection.


Avoid direct sun exposure: Protect the treated area from direct sunlight or tanning beds for up to a month to prevent hyperpigmentation.


Gentle cleansing: Clean the area with a gentle cleanser and lukewarm water. Avoid harsh soaps or scrubs.


Common questions

Is vaginal bleaching safe?

+

Yes, vaginal bleaching is safe when performed by trained professionals using FDA-approved treatments. At VidaSpaNYC, we ensure that all procedures are conducted in a sterile, controlled environment with advanced products and technologies.

How long does it take to see results?

+

Results vary depending on the method used, but most clients notice improvements after 1–3 sessions. Laser treatments often show quicker results, while topical treatments may require more sessions for gradual improvement.

Will the effect be permanent?

+

Results are long-lasting but not permanent. Maintenance treatments and proper skincare can help preserve the results.


Does the procedure hurt?

+

The procedure is designed to be pain-free. Topical treatments may cause mild tingling, while laser treatments are generally painless. A topical anesthetic can be used to ensure comfort.
